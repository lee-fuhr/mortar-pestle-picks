A granite mortar and pestle is the workhorse of any serious cook’s countertop. Unlike porous materials, polished granite won’t absorb oils or odors, and its density gives you the heft needed to crush spices, seeds, and herbs with minimal effort. The key factors to consider are bowl capacity (think about how much you typically grind at once), interior roughness (a lightly textured surface grips ingredients better than a mirror-smooth one), and pestle length, a longer handle puts more leverage behind each stroke. Size and weight matter too: a mortar that’s too light will slide around, while one that’s too heavy becomes a chore to lift and clean. Choose based on your cooking volume and the types of grinding you do most often.
